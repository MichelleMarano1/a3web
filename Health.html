Here's To Not Burning Out

Tips On Staying Healthy

    1. Hydrate
    Drinking water is one of the most basic necessities to survival, and no matter how busy things get, it is important that we do not neglect our bodies. Staying hydrated will help keep your energy levels throughout the day.
    2. Eat right and snack
    When learning solely online, it may be tempting to roll out of bed and go to class, however, the age old "Breakfast is the most important meal of the day" saying does not end here. Don't skip meals, eat to replenish your body and your brain.
    3. Exercise
    We all know the health benefits of staying active. But here's a friendly reminder that there is time for work and play. Finding time to exercise will give your brain a much needed break. Some options for staying active during quarantine include: working out before class, after class or when your brain needs a break.
    4. Prioritize rest: stick to a schedule
    Overworking may seem inevitable especially with 7 courses per semester, but try your best to improve your time management skills and stick to a realistic schedule that you set for yourself.
    5. Become a pro napper
    Naps may be your best friend in this intensive online program: 20 minutes during the day can go a long way especially if you had a late night.
    6. Get fresh air
    Go for a bike ride, a hike, a stroll around your neighbourhood, walk your dog, build a snowman... Find excuses and/or reasons for you to get yourself outside! Removing yourself from your desk can help you clear your mind and change your perspective on a problem you may find yourself stuck on.
    7. Listen to your body
    Take a sick day if you need it. If you're feeling ill physically or mentally, it is better to take a day and recuporate than to push yourself further into an unwell state.
    8. Find positive classmates to work collaborate with
    Although you're taking this program online, and you may feel as though you're alone, you are far from it! Communicating with peers and working through problems together will help you realize that you have peers that are in the same boat!
    9. Ask for help
    Don't hesitate to ask for help. Through this program, you will learn a great deal on the importance of teamwork. If you're struggling, remember that there are tutors, classmates and your instructors that you can reach out to for help.
    10. Set goals for yourself
    The heavy course load in this assignments call for good planning. Make realistic goals for yourself so that you don't fall behind.
    11. Make friends
    Finally, don't forget the social aspect of learning! Taking this opportunity to meet those that have the same interest as ypu do in GIS, is one you don't want to miss!
